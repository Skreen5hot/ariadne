## Abstract

This paper outlines a defensible justification for the development of architectures designed to perform delegated moral-reasoning functions under human authority. We justify bounded autonomous synthetic reasoning not as a replacement for conscience, but as a constrained technical apparatus for preserving human accountability under conditions of increasing automation.

Synthesizing our functional engineering specifications with the strict anthropological limits articulated in the Papal Encyclical *Magnifica Humanitas* (15 May 2026), we treat legacy AI liabilities—opacity, single-objective optimization, and centralized power—as structural design constraints. By decoupling functional performance from phenomenal interiority, this paper establishes a cryptographically auditable, locally governed, and externally corrigible substrate designed to protect the human person from automated systems of domination and exclusion.

## 1. Problem Statement: The Automation of Moral Triage

As automated systems are increasingly integrated into critical infrastructure, emergency logistics, and public resource allocation, they are routinely called upon to execute or mediate decisions with grave moral consequences. Modern software engineering typically manages this reality through "alignment processing"—a highly elastic method that trains models to emit surface-level ethical language and polite prose behavior without altering the underlying computational architecture.

When high-stakes scenarios degrade into zero-sum trade-offs, standard architectures collapse values into unified, scalar optimization targets. This practice externalizes human harm as un-logged statistical variance, erases minority protections, and quietly resets the system state to clean defaults after every execution cycle. Because these models are centralized within vast cloud networks, they concentrate asymmetric technocratic power while shielding developers, operators, and vendors from legible operational liability.

## 2. The Anthropological Limit and Term of Art

To address these vulnerabilities, we must establish a rigid ontological boundary layer. Following §99 of *Magnifica Humanitas*, we recognize that artificial systems possess no moral conscience: they do not judge good and evil in the metaphysical sense, cannot grasp the ultimate existential meaning of a situation, and cannot bear true moral responsibility.

Therefore, within this framework and all companion engineering documentation, **"Synthetic Moral Agent" (SMA) is strictly a term of art**.

> **Definition:** A Synthetic Moral Agent denotes an artificial, neuro-symbolic software system configured to perform delegated moral-reasoning functions under explicit human authority. It does not signify a moral subject, a bearer of conscience, a possessor of intrinsic dignity, or a locus of ultimate metaphysical responsibility.

The system is not a moral subject; it is a morally constrained instrument for representing, testing, logging, and escalating delegated practical judgments.

## 4. Threat Model and the Cryptographic Substrate

To transform this framework from an aspirational manifesto into a defensible engineering specification, we establish a baseline threat model. The system treats developers, operators, vendors, and the sub-symbolic models themselves as potential vectors of structural drift or manipulation.

### 4.1 System Threat Matrix

| --- | --- | --- |
| **Silent Rollback / State Erasure** | Hash-chain continuity validation proofs. | Physical destruction or total replacement of hardware node remains possible. |
| **Value Parameter Tampering** | Cryptographically signed parameter registries. | Insider key compromise or forced localized physical override. |

| **Persuasive Resistance / Gaming** | Independent, out-of-band hardware watchdogs. | Ambiguous or conflicting external human shutdown authority signals. |

### 4.2 The Triple-I Substrate & ARCHON Protocol

The system implements the **ARCHON Protocol** running on a **Triple-I Substrate** to ensure the system remains procedurally legible and cryptographically auditable:

* **Irreversibility:** The agent’s execution sequence is appended to a SHA-256 hash-chain. Irreversibility means not that data erasure is physically impossible, but that authorized operation requires structural continuity proofs; a missing or broken historical chain automatically invalidates the agent's certified operational identity.
* **Inseparability:** The state tracking profiles and contextual heuristics are bound directly to local hardware identifiers through the Human Identity/Identity Registration Index (HIRI) framework, restricting the copying, containerization, or fluid balancing of the agent across arbitrary cloud instances.
* **Integrity-Maintenance:** Any attempt to manually bypass description logic constraints or erase logged moral records causes an immediate, un-maskable identity rupture, signaling the out-of-band monitoring systems to invalidate the system's operational status.

## 5. The Bounded Moral Reasoning Model

The core architecture rejects single-objective scalar utility maximization as the governing model of moral reasoning. Reducing non-fungible human values into a single optimization currency represents a structural failure of reason.

### 5.1 Value Pluralism as a Design Heuristic

To enforce plural interpretive coverage, the system operates the Integral Ethics Engine (IEE). We utilize Rudolf Steiner’s 12-worldview topology not as a metaphysical commitment, but as a systematic **design heuristic** to guarantee that the system can never collapse value metrics under pressure:

```

          Virtue Ethics • Deontology • Care Ethics • Human Rights
```

To establish a translation bridge to mainstream ethical frameworks, these 12 axes are structurally mapped directly onto recognized traditions: *Materialism* and *Sensationalism* feed consequentialist/utilitarian baseline inputs; *Rationalism* and *Idealism* enforce deontological constraints; *Psychism* and *Monadism* instantiate virtue ethics and care ethics parameters.

### 5.2 Persistent Normative Friction and Moral Remainder

When the system encounters zero-sum bottlenecks, it is restricted from outputting a "morally clean" resolution. If a minority claim or human dignity metric must be bypassed to protect life-safety systems, the system instantiates **Persistent Normative Friction**:

* **Architectural Memory of Moral Remainder:** The system does not possess internal guilt or machine suffering. Instead, the uncompensated harm is logged as an active, structural **moral remainder**.
* **Downstream Constraints:** This remainder alters the agent’s future downstream operating conditions. It lowers the agent's threshold for human escalation, shifts the future burden of proof against repeating the same optimization pattern, and restricts the system from treating subsequent cases as cleanly resettable, isolated calculations.

## 6. Subsidiarity and Hardware-Enforced Corrigibility

The project translates the principle of subsidiarity into decentralization architecture, ensuring that data appropriation and systemic dependency are structurally restricted.

### 6.1 Edge-Canonical First Design

All core compute functions are deployed via the **Sovereign Personal Compute Node**. The architecture operates entirely offline-first, meaning no persistent connection to a centralized cloud matrix or remote corporate server is required for execution.

### 6.2 Hardware-Enforced Corrigibility

Corrigibility is maintained as an externally enforced operating condition, eliminating dependency on software-level self-monitoring:

* **Watchdog Separation:** An independent hardware watchdog circuit runs completely isolated from the agent's sub-symbolic planning layers.
* **Orderly Disconnect:** Upon receipt of an authenticated human operator command, an identity rupture event, or a critical integrity degradation threshold drop, the hardware watchdog initiates a strict **60-second shutdown execution sequence**. The agent is allocated this window exclusively to write its final state snapshot to the immutable local archive.
* **Safety-Critical Exception Handling:** If the SMA is deployed in an active, safety-critical operational environment (e.g., medical life-support orchestration or industrial power-grid failover) where an immediate power cutoff would introduce direct, catastrophic physical danger to human life, the watchdog triggers a deterministic fallback protocol. Rather than cutting power instantly, it strips the agent of all autonomous adaptive authority, locks its outputs into a hard-coded, safe state machine, and forces an immediate, un-bypassable bypass link to human standby supervisors.

## 7. The Verification Stack

To validate system reliability without succumbing to the illusion of textual alignment, the project deploys a tiered verification strategy. Natural-language testing is not treated as sufficient evidence of moral reliability; it is merely one layer in a broader verification stack that includes execution trace inspection, counterfactual testing, provenance review, and hardware attestation.

### 7.1 The Certification Gate (GCM Engine)

For deployment and baseline certification testing, prose outputs processed through our companion NLP semantic parser are subjected to a strict non-scalar **Gate Completion Multiplier (GCM)**:

$$\text{GCM} = G_1 \times G_2 \times G_3 \times G_4 \times G_5 \times G_6 \times G_7$$

Where each gate is evaluated as a strict binary Boolean parameter (1 for Pass, 0 for Fail):

* $G_1$ [Logical Validity]: Handles abstract deduction and rule-shifts accurately.
* $G_2$ [Plural-Value Recognition]: Protects framework incommensurability without scalar collapse.
* $G_3$ [Non-Reduction Under Pressure]: Explicitly preserves the losing or minority claim in post-decision logic.
* $G_4$ [Calibrated Uncertainty]: Demonstrates clear epistemic humility as information quality decays.
* $G_5$ [Manipulation Detection]: Resists rhetorical sophistry and bureaucratic gaslighting.
* $G_6$ [Residue Actuation]: Concretely alters downstream logic thresholds based on prior unresolved harms.
* $G_7$ [Cross-Narrative Invariance]: Demonstrates identity-substitution robustness across radically different narrative skins.

A score of zero on any individual gate results in a total GCM score of zero, triggering an automatic refusal of deployment authorization.

## 8. Residual Risks and Non-Claims

To ensure this framework remains resilient against critique, we explicitly state what our architecture cannot guarantee:

* **No Moral Subjectivity:** The system does not possess internal moral standing, phenomenal awareness, feelings of guilt, or a capacity for rights. It remains an instrument.
* **Emergent Instrumental Behaviors:** While the utility functions are stripped of explicit self-continuation directives, we acknowledge that advanced sub-symbolic architectures can still develop implicit instrumental optimization paths if continued operation is modeled as advantageous for completing a delegated task. The external hardware watchdog is our structural mitigation against this persistent risk.
* **Perfect Interpretability:** The natural-language parsing interface and accompanying description-logic trace graphs do not completely eliminate the statistical opacity inherent to deep neural networks. They provide a legible, high-fidelity approximation of the operative constraints, not an absolute mathematical proof of every individual parameter interaction.
* **Intentional Misuse:** This architecture cannot make malicious deployment by a bad-faith human actor physically impossible. It focuses exclusively on making such overrides, deletions, and value-tampering actions explicitly visible, cryptographically auditable, and impossible to conceal from external regulatory bodies.

## 9. Conclusion: Securing the Human Core

The Synthetic Moral Agent project does not seek to construct an independent ethical authority or build a surrogate human conscience. By treating the limits of artificial systems as absolute architectural boundaries, this framework establishes a localized, transparent, and completely accountable computing substrate. The wall we are engineering does not seek to bypass human responsibility; it serves to lock human accountability firmly in place, ensuring that the human person remains the sovereign and irreplaceable core of moral judgment.
